What is Airlaid?

What is airlaid and how is it different from regular tissue?

Airlaid is a dry-laid nonwoven material made from dispersed cellulose fibers and bonded for performance. Compared with ordinary tissue paper napkins, airlaid is thicker, softer, more absorbent, and more durable, making it a better fit for restaurants, hotels, cafés, and premium dining service.

For Indian dining setups, where guests often use napkins with hands, sauces, gravies, oils, and beverages, airlaid offers a more practical and premium solution than standard paper napkins.

Material benefits

Softness, absorbency, strength, and foodservice usability

  • Softness: Gentle, fabric-like feel that elevates table presentation.
  • Absorbency: Handles spills, sauces, and oils more effectively than regular tissue.
  • Strength: Resists tearing during service, handling, and folding.
  • Hygiene: Single-use format supports cleaner, safer dining operations.
  • Versatility: Suitable for dine-in service, buffets, banquets, and catered events.
