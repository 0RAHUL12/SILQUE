About SILQUE

SILQUE is a Bengaluru-based airlaid napkin brand focused on premium 60-75 GSM napkins for hotels, restaurants, cafes, caterers, events, banquets, corporates, and distributors.

Why SILQUE exists

SILQUE was created to bring a more refined single-use dining standard to Indian hospitality. Many professional dining teams want the hygiene and operational convenience of disposable napkins, but standard paper napkins often feels too light for premium service. SILQUE focuses on airlaid material because it offers better softness, absorbency, texture, and table presence.

The brand is built for buyers who notice small details: how a napkin sits on the table, how it feels in hand, how it supports cutlery presentation, and whether it keeps service consistent across repeated use.

Sustainability

Airlaid dry-laid technology for professional dining.

SILQUE focuses on fibre-based 60-75 GSM airlaid napkins and efficient single-use formats that help hospitality teams maintain hygiene and presentation without depending on cloth laundering for every service moment.

Airlaid technology forms the material by distributing fibres with air instead of using water as the carrying medium in conventional wet-laid paper production. Industry sources report that premium airlaid napkins can use up to 95% less water during production than conventional paper napkins.

For hospitality buyers, the practical impact is lower production water demand, no large water-evaporation step, minimal wastewater, and a more responsible material choice for high-volume table service. Total carbon footprint still depends on sourcing, energy, logistics, and disposal, so SILQUE keeps the claim focused on the dry-laid airlaid process and responsible procurement.

External industry references: Duni Group explains airlaid material for hospitality use; Origin Sustainables discusses reduced-water airlaid production.
