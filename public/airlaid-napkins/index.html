What Are Airlaid Napkins?

Airlaid napkins are premium cellulose-based nonwoven napkins designed for softness, absorbency, hygiene, and a cloth-like dining presence.

Material Difference

Airlaid is chosen when standard paper napkins is not enough.

Airlaid is a dry-laid, cellulose-based nonwoven material. In hospitality napkins, it is valued because it can feel thicker, softer, and more fabric-like than standard tissue while staying hygienic and single-use. SILQUE works in the premium 60-75 GSM airlaid range for buyers who want a substantial table feel.

For buyers, the difference is visible in the texture, fold quality, absorbency, and table presence. A good airlaid napkin should not collapse visually on the table or feel too light in hand. It should support beverage service, full-course dining, buffet operations, and branded events.

Softness

Airlaid napkins are selected for a softer handfeel than standard paper napkins, making them suitable for guest-facing dining environments.

Absorbency

The 60-75 GSM airlaid range supports beverage and meal service where a thin paper napkin may feel inadequate.

Presentation

Airlaid holds a more premium visual presence on plates, trays, buffets, and formal table settings.
