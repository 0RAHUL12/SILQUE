What Is Airlaid

What Is Airlaid?

Imagine a napkin that feels like premium linen cloth, but is completely disposable. That is Airlaid: the softness of fabric combined with the convenience of paper.

01. Manufacturing Process

How Airlaid is Engineered: The Dry-Laid System

Unlike standard tissue paper which uses huge volumes of water to carry fibers (the wet-laid method), Airlaid is manufactured using a clean dry-laid system. By using air currents, the fibers are formed into a soft, textile-like material that is both robust and gentle.

1

Air Dispersion

100% virgin cellulose wood pulp fibers are separated into individual fibers using rapid, pressurized air streams instead of water suspension.

2

Forming the Web

The air-dispersed fibers are laid down onto a continuous moving forming mesh, creating a uniform, highly fluffy fiber web structure.

3

Thermal Bonding

The web is bonded together using heat and an eco-friendly binder. This locks the wood fibers into a strong, highly absorbent cloth-like sheet.

02. Properties & Characteristics

Exceptional Absorption & Tear Strength

Due to the airy, dry-laid structure, Airlaid material has an incredibly high void volume. This allows it to absorb liquids rapidly and retain up to 12 times its own weight in fluid without breaking down.

04. Environmental Benefits

Sustainable Operations without Compromise

True sustainability makes sense operationally. SILQUE Airlaid reduces the daily environmental load of linen laundering and tissue waste simultaneously.

95% Water Saving

The dry-laid manufacturing process uses air currents instead of water, saving up to 95% of fresh water compared to conventional wet-laid papermaking mills.

Reduced Waste Volume

Because Airlaid napkins are thick and highly absorbent, guests use only 1 napkin per meal. Standard thin tissues require multiple sheets, multiplying waste volume.

Biodegradable Fibers

SILQUE Airlaid is made of 100% natural, plant-based wood cellulose. The fibers naturally decompose back into the earth, minimizing landfill footprint.
