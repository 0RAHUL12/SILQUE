What Is Airlaid

What Is Airlaid?

Airlaid is a soft, absorbent, paper-like material used for premium napkins. It feels fuller than ordinary tissue while staying different from cloth.

Material Difference

Airlaid sits between ordinary tissue and cloth.

Airlaid material is made by distributing cellulose fibres with air and bonding them into a soft, absorbent, paper-like sheet. For table napkins, that creates a surface that feels fuller than thin tissue but does not have the weave, stitching, or laundry requirement of cloth.

Hospitality buyers choose airlaid because it improves table presentation, fold quality, handfeel, and absorbency while keeping single-use hygiene practical for repeated service.

Reference: Duni Group article on what airlaid is.

Why It Works

Better table feel without cloth handling.

Not Thin Tissue

Airlaid has more body and absorbency than basic tissue napkins.

Not Cloth

It remains a paper-like nonwoven material without woven fabric texture, stitching, or laundering.

Premium Table Feel

It supports a cleaner fold and a more substantial dining impression.
