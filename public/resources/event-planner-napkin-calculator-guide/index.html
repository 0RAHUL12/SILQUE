Banquet Sizing Sourcing Checklists

Choosing the correct size is just as important as ordering the right volume. Applying the wrong format can slow down table prep or leave tables looking empty:

  • 8x8 Cocktail Napkin (20cm x 20cm open): Fits bar counters, tray-passed welcome drinks, lounge seating, and desserts. For cocktails and high-density events, estimate a higher multiplier (1.5 to 2.0 per guest) because guests take a new napkin with each fresh beverage.
  • 16x16 Dinner Napkin (40cm x 40cm open): Best for plated banquets, formal corporate lunches, and wedding covers. It holds its fold shape (e.g., pyramid, pocket, or crown) on the cover. Estimate 1.2 per guest (allowing a small buffer for drop replacements).
  • Pocket Cutlery Sleeve (pre-folded 1/8 book fold): Specifically designed for buffets, room service, and outdoor catering. The pre-folded pocket holds flatware together. Because the fork and knife are pre-wrapped, you only need 1.0 napkin per guest, cutting setup times by up to 50%.

Guide on Sourcing banquet napkins & catering napkins

When procuring bulk napkins for events, plan for these timelines and checks:

  1. Confirm the Theme and Colors: Woven-textured airlaid napkins are available in hospitality-grade colors. Pick ivory or white for formal banquets, burgundy for weddings, navy for corporate, or charcoal for modern themes.
  2. Verify Lead Times for Custom Prints: Custom print plates take time to prepare. Plan for a 7 to 10 day dispatch lead time from the Bangalore warehouse for custom branded orders.
  3. Establish a Sourcing Buffer: Always add a 10% buffer to your guest count calculation. Emergency banquet rushes can deplete stock, and having backup boxes ready in your storage room is essential.
